রংতুলি

রংতুলি, located in Dhaka, is dedicated to showcasing the artistry of individuals. Providing them with a platform to both create and sell their products. By providing both skills and opportunities, we wish to uplift the community — making sure everyone lives with dignity and purpose.

Learn More About Our Mission

Despite being the fastest developing city, Dhaka still faces challenges.

“Poverty in Bangladesh has surged over the past three years, with nearly 28 percent of the population now poor compared to 18.7 percent in 2022, according to new estimates by the Power and Participation Research Centre.”
  • Limited Access to Education
  • Overpopulation
  • Unemployment
“Bangladesh is facing an 'employment emergency' as 38 percent of those counted as employed are actually underemployed, working less than 40 hours a week” – The Daily Star

Most individuals work tirelessly as rickshaw pullers, labourers, or domestic workers. Despite their hard work, they earn too little. The poorest 10 percent earn just Tk 8,477 a month but spend Tk 12,294, often through borrowing or external support.

  1. Training: 6-month courses with experienced tailors to teach stitching and designing skills.
  2. Funding: Financial support through donations and micro-loans to buy tools and materials.
  3. Resources: Access to machinery, workspace, delivery platforms to focus on learning.
  4. Online Platform: Connect trainees directly with buyers to grow sustainably.
  1. Ability: Skills like stitching and tailoring for starting a small business.
  2. Computing: Hands-on computer training for updating stock and pricing.
  3. Accounting: Lessons in bookkeeping and managing finances efficiently.
  4. Delivery Services: Training in logistics, customer handling, and e-commerce operations.
  1. Workspace Setup: A workspace will be rented near the trainees’ communities for easy access and convenience.
  2. Youth Involvement: Youths from the trainees’ households will be trained in accounting and delivery, creating additional job opportunities.
  3. Resource Support: All essential materials — including sewing machines, threads, fabrics, and electricity — will be provided to remove financial pressure. Our estimated startup cost is 2.8 lakh (~5000 Tk per trainee). This includes second-hand refurbished sewing machines, six-month subsidized workspace access, and trainee salary. To secure this, we have a hybrid funding model: 70% from NGOs/UN bodies, 30% from the government, plus community contributions.
  4. Commission Policy: Only a 10% commission will be taken for the first 500 pieces produced to help trainees become financially stable. This serves as a Training Cost Recovery Fund for the organization, allowing us to recoup the initial investment per trainee. Once the trainee is certified and profitable, they shift to work independently.
